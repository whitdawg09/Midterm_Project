Conservation Efforts

The Importance of Shark Conservation

Sharks are keystone species that play an essential role in oceanic and coastal communities. Because they are apex predators, they stabilize food webs and act as a barometer for ocean health. Thus, protecting them will also help significantly with broader conservation issues, such as protecting our ocean’s health, the world’s food security, and the economic stability of hundreds of millions of people.

What Conservation Efforts are in Process

  • Marine Protected Areas (MPAs): Shark populations thrive in fully protected areas, especially when combined with national policies that restrict destructive fishing practices
  • Fisheries Management: Many organizations are tightening regulations on shark finning and bycatch reduction to protect vulnerable species like Oceanic Whitetips and Threshers
  • Monitoring Programs: Global efforts like the FinPrint project use underwater cameras to track shark populations and guide conservation strategies based on alarming declines

How Can You Help?

Reduce Single-Use Plastics Make Sustainable Seafood Choices Say No to Shark Products Support Conservation Organizations Spread Awareness
Plastic waste often ends up in oceans, harming sharks and other marine life. Using reusable bags, bottles, and containers helps keep the oceans cleaner Avoid seafood from sources that contribute to overfishing or bycatch of sharks. Use guides like the Marine Stewardship Council (MSC) or Seafood Watch for sustainable options Avoid buying products made from sharks, such as shark fin soup or shark cartilage supplements, which contribute to unsustainable harvesting Donating or volunteering with organizations like the Shark League or WWF supports ongoing efforts to protect shark species Educating others about the importance of sharks for ocean ecosystems can reduce misconceptions and inspire conservation actions. Social media campaigns and petitions are easy ways to get involved
