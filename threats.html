Threats to Sharks

Many Species of Sharks are Endangered

According to the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), 31.2% of shark species are threatened with extinction. This includes 11% of sharks that are endangered, 12% that are critically endangered, and 14% that are vulnerable

Main threats include: Overfishing, Habitat Destruction, Climate Change, and Shark Finning

Shark Tangled in Net

Overfishing

Overfishing is the biggest threat to most larger reef sharks. This is because overfishing depletes the shark's food supply, disrupting their natural diet.

Habitat Destruction

Damage to reefs and other key habitats, such as mangroves, which are used as nursery grounds by some shark species.

Climate Change

Climate Change is warming ocean waters and disrupting the migration patterns and habitats of sharks, pushing them to less suitable environments. It also threatens biodiversity, which threatens shark populations that are dependent on healthy ecosystems.

Shark Finning

A growing trade in shark fins, which are often used to make an expensive Asian soup, has also been a serious threat to shark populations. An estimated 100 million sharks are killed annually for their fins.
